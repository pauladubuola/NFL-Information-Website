NFL Equipment

Players who play in the NFL have plenty of equipment while they are playing on the field. But thier equipment can mainly consist of:

  • A Helmet
  • Shoulder Pads
  • Thigh and Knee Pads
  • Mouthguard
  • Gloves

Helmets

There are many types of helmets that NFL players can wear, as long as they meet the league's guidelines. But when choosing a helmet, players consider factors like: Fit, Comfort, Durability, Player position, and the player's medical history. The NFL also prohibits certain helmets, including:
  • Discontinued helmets that were worn by less than 1% of players for the past two seasons
  • Helmets that perform poorly in laboratory testing
  • Helmets that have been discontinued by the manufacturer
  • Helmets that were produced by companies no longer manufacturing football helmets


Shoulder Pads

A properly fitting shoulder pad shouldn't pinch your neck or collar, and should sit comfortably on your shoulders.


Thigh and Knee Pads

Thigh and knee pads are primarily used to protect the respective areas of the leg from impact injuries, particularly during activities that involve frequent kneeling or collisions.


Mouthguards

A mouth guard protects football players from injury to their teeth, gums, jaw, and other parts of their face during play
